Mid Autumn

Festival

a brief history

The Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋節) is a harvest festival celebrated notably by the Chinese and Vietnamese people. It relates to Chuseok (in Korea) and Tsukimi (in Japan).

In China, the Mid-Autumn festival symbolizes the family reunion and family reunion, and on this day, all families will appreciate the moon in the evening, because it is the 15th day of the eighth month of the lunar calendar, when the moon is at its fullest. There is a beautiful myth about the Mid-Autumn festival, that is Chang'e flying to the moon. Offerings are also made to the lunar deity, Chang'e, known as the Moon Goddess of Immortality.

Entertainment - We're Here to Have Fun!

  • the lion dance

    Led by the members of the AAU (Asian Americans Unite), the lion dance is a traditional Chinese performance in which “lions” perform on the streets and go to houses asking for permission to perform for them. If the host consents, the "lion" will come in and start dancing as a blessing of luck and fortune for the home.

  • martial art demonstration

    Led also by the members of the AAU (Asian Americans Unite), members will be performing and demonstrating various forms of chinese martial arts, or kung fu, on Arch and 9th Street starting at 2:00pm.

Food - The Yummy Menu!

  • mooncake

    “The round moon cakes are symbols of the great family reunion just like the round.” There will be many varieties and flavors of mooncakes available for both sampling and purchase. You will also get the chance to make your own!

  • duck

    Duck is the second most common food after mooncakes in the Mid-Autumn Festival. Chinese people believe that eating duck in autumn can expel pathogenic heat from their body to keep the balance between yin and yang, thus to enhance health.

  • crab

    Hairy Crab is a Shanghai specialty that is often eaten for Mid Autumn Festival because these crabs are "in season" in September and October. Around this time of the year, the crabs are getting ready to lay their eggs, which makes them the tastiest according to Chinese people. They are highly abundant in amino acids and protein.
